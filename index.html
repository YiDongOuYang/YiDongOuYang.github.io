About

Hey, I am Yidong Ouyang, a Ph.D. candidate at UCLA, where I am advised by Guang Cheng. Prior to this, I earned my MPhil from CUHK Shenzhen, where I was advised by Liyan Xie. My research interests lie in generative models, especially diffusion and flow models.

Publications

* denotes equal contribution.

  1. dFlowGRPO: Rate-Aware Policy Optimization for Discrete Flow Models
    Zhengyan Wan, Yidong Ouyang, Panwen Hu, Qiang Sun
    [arxiv] [code]
    We derive the full trajectory probability for DFMs and formulate denoising as a Markov decision process, enabling dFlowGRPO to incorporate information from both the associated conditional transition rates and the posterior model during reinforcement learning.

  2. Training-Free Self-Correction for Multimodal Masked Diffusion Models
    Yidong Ouyang, Panwen Hu, Zhengyan Wan, Zhe Wang, Liyan Xie, Dmitriy Bespalov, Ying Nian Wu, Guang Cheng, Hongyuan Zha, Qiang Sun
    [arxiv] [code]
    We propose a training-free self-correction framework that exploits the inductive biases of pre-trained masked diffusion models. Without modifying model parameters or introducing auxiliary evaluators, our method significantly improves generation quality on text-to-image generation and multimodal understanding tasks with reduced sampling steps.

  3. Corrected Samplers for Discrete Flow Models
    Zhengyan Wan, Yidong Ouyang, Liyan Xie, Fang Fang, Hongyuan Zha, Guang Cheng
    International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2026
    [arxiv] [code]
    We propose two corrected samplers: a time-corrected sampler and a location-corrected sampler, which reduce the discretization error of tau-leaping and Euler solvers with almost no additional computational cost.

  4. Discrete Guidance Matching: Exact Guidance for Discrete Flow Matching
    Yidong Ouyang*, Zhengyan Wan*, Liyan Xie, Fang Fang, Hongyuan Zha, Guang Cheng
    International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR), 2026
    [arxiv] [code]
    We derive the exact transition rate for the desired distribution given a learned discrete flow matching model, leading to guidance that only requires a single forward pass in each sampling step.

  5. Error Analysis of Discrete Flow with Generator Matching
    Zhengyan Wan, Yidong Ouyang, Qiang Yao, Liyan Xie, Fang Fang, Hongyuan Zha, Guang Cheng
    International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2026
    [arxiv]
    We derive the KL divergence between two path measures of continuous-time Markov chains with different transition rates by developing a novel Girsanov-type theorem, and provide a comprehensive analysis of the error arising from transition rate estimation and early stopping.

  6. Alignment of Diffusion Model and Flow Matching for Text-to-Image Generation
    Yidong Ouyang, Liyan Xie, Hongyuan Zha, Guang Cheng
    [arxiv]
    We propose a novel alignment framework by leveraging the underlying nature of the alignment problem—sampling from reward-weighted distributions—and show that it applies to both diffusion models (via score guidance) and flow matching models (via velocity guidance).

  7. Transfer Learning for Diffusion Models
    Yidong Ouyang, Liyan Xie, Hongyuan Zha, Guang Cheng
    Annual Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS), 2024
    [arxiv] [code]
    We prove that the optimal diffusion model for the target domain integrates pre-trained diffusion models on the source domain with additional guidance from a domain classifier. We further extend TGDP to a conditional version for modeling the joint distribution of data and labels, together with two additional regularization terms.

  8. MissDiff: Training Diffusion Models on Tabular Data with Missing Values
    Yidong Ouyang, Liyan Xie, Chongxuan Li, Guang Cheng
    ICML Workshop on Structured Probabilistic Inference & Generative Modeling, 2023
    [arxiv] [code]
    We observe that the widely adopted “impute-then-generate” pipeline may lead to a biased learning objective, and propose to mask the regression loss of denoising score matching during training. We prove the proposed method is consistent in learning the score of data distributions.

  9. Improving Adversarial Robustness Through the Contrastive-Guided Diffusion Process
    Yidong Ouyang, Liyan Xie, Guang Cheng
    International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2023
    [arxiv] [code]
    We analyze the optimality condition of the synthetic distribution for achieving non-trivial robust accuracy, and propose the Contrastive-Guided Diffusion Process (Contrastive-DP), which adopts a contrastive loss to guide the diffusion model in data generation.

  10. Attention Enables Zero Approximation Error
    Zhiying Fang, Yidong Ouyang, Ding-Xuan Zhou, Guang Cheng
    [arxiv]
    We theoretically prove that the self-attention model can achieve zero approximation error, and that our proposed model can avoid the classical trade-off between approximation error and sample error in the mean squared error analysis.

  11. Generalizing to Unseen Domains: A Survey on Domain Generalization
    Jindong Wang, Cuiling Lan, Chang Liu, Yidong Ouyang, Tao Qin
    IEEE TKDE / IJCAI 2021 survey track
    [arxiv]
    The first review of recent advances in domain generalization, including the theory of generalization, a taxonomy of domain generalization methods, and potential research topics.

  12. Learning Efficient Convolutional Networks through Irregular Convolutional Kernels
    Weiyu Guo, Jiabin Ma, Yidong Ouyang, Liang Wang, Yongzhen Huang
    Neurocomputing
    [paper]
    We propose RotateConv kernels, an interpolation-based method that transforms traditional square kernels into line segments, massively reducing the number of parameters and calculations while maintaining acceptable performance.
